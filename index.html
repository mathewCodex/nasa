Welcome to the Phytoplankton World

Phytoplankton are the autotrophic components of the plankton community and a key part of ocean and freshwater ecosystems. The name comes from the Greek words φυτόν, meaning 'plant', and πλαγκτός, meaning 'wanderer' or 'drifter'. Phytoplankton obtain their energy through photosynthesis, as do trees and other plants on land

Explore The Ocean

Imagine you visit a landlocked region where none of the inhabitants have ever been to the ocean. Although they may not be aware of it, every single day these people breathe oxygen generated by marine photosynthesis, benefit from the lowered atmospheric carbon dioxide (CO2) levels induced by marine phytoplankton, and consume food that was fueled by marine ecosystems. In essence, the ocean can be viewed as a garden that provides beauty, resources, and CO2 drawdown for society. Dinoflagellate Matic

The dinoflagellates are a monophyletic group of single-celled eukaryotes constituting the phylum Dinoflagellata and are usually considered protist. Dinoflagellates are mostly marine plankton, but they also are common in freshwater habitats. Their populations vary with sea surface temperature, salinity, and depth

Green Algae

The green algae are a group consisting of the Prasinodermophyta and its unnamed sister which contains the Chlorophyta and Charophyta/Streptophyta. The land plants have emerged deep in the Charophyte alga as sister of the Zygnematophyceae. Since the realization that the Embryophytes emerged within the green algae, some authors are starting to include them

Green Algae

Characteristics

Green algae are a diverse group of photosynthetic organisms, primarily found in aquatic environments, including freshwater and marine habitats

Chloroplasts: Green algae contain chlorophyll, which gives them their green color and allows them to photosynthesize. Biodiversity: They are highly diverse, with over 7,000 known species, ranging from simple unicellular organisms to complex multicellular structures

Dinoflagellate

Characteristics

Dinoflagellates are single-celled, aquatic microorganisms belonging to the group of protists. They are characterized by their unique whip-like appendages called flagella.

Microscopic Organisms: Dinoflagellates are tiny, unicellular organisms, typically ranging from 20 to 200 micrometers in size. Aquatic Habitat: They inhabit marine and freshwater environments, and some are even found in symbiotic relationships with other organisms.

Diatom

Characteristics

Diatoms are microscopic, single-celled algae found in various aquatic environments. They play a crucial role in ecosystems and have several distinctive features

Photosynthesis: They are primary producers, using photosynthesis to convert light energy into organic matter. Fossil Fuels: Over millions of years, diatoms have contributed to the formation of fossil fuels like diatomaceous earth.
